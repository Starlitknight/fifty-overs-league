# Live-match city art prompt system

## Shared invariant

Create a polished 16:9 live-match environment for *Fifty Overs*. It must depict a real cricket play in progress, including a striker, wicketkeeper correctly behind the striker's stumps, non-striker, bowler, correctly placed umpires and plausible fielders. Show at most one ball. Use accurate cricket geometry and body physics.

Render it as premium mature sports-anime art for an adult strategy game: expressive but controlled linework, clear cel-shaded light planes, selective gouache texture, cinematic depth, accurate architecture and energetic but credible player poses. Avoid photorealism, childish proportions, chibi, glossy 3D, copied franchise design, stadium spectacle, readable generated text, logos and real clubs.

Each city must change all six dimensions below rather than merely changing landmarks:

1. Camera height and lens.
2. Direction of the pitch within the frame.
3. Specific cricket event.
4. Time, cloud and wind.
5. Emotional promise.
6. Position of the quiet interface-safe region.

## City treatments

### Dublin — Morning Cover Drive

High-oblique scorebox viewpoint; pitch cuts diagonally; batter times a grounded cover drive; cool early morning and long soft shadows; Georgian brick, pale civic stone and bus beyond; emotion is elegance and control.

### Belfast — Into the Wind

Grass-level camera just outside the rope behind a fast bowler's final strides; hard dry gusts and racing slate-white cloud; linen-mill brick, hill and shipyard engineering; emotion is pressure and defiance.

### Cork — Six Over the River

View from the opposite quay across the River Lee; one lofted ball hangs above the riverside ground while fielders run; warm golden hour, colorful hillside and harbor masts; emotion is communal suspense and release.

### Amsterdam — The Keeper's View

Low three-quarter viewpoint immediately behind the keeper; batter advances to a finger spinner; crisp high summer noon; canal bridge, bicycles, tram and gabled houses; emotion is wit, footwork and tactical nerve.

### Utrecht — The Impossible Single

High bird's-eye view from an old rooftop; two batters risk a quick single as a close fielder attacks the ball; still amber evening, canal wharves and long geometric shadows; emotion is quiet calculation turning suddenly dangerous.

### Rotterdam — Last Over Against the Light

Low side-on square-leg viewpoint; fast bowler releases in the closing over; luminous windy harbor sunset, modern towers, converted warehouses, bridge and cranes; emotion is acceleration and future-facing ambition.
