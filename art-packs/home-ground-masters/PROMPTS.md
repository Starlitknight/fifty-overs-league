These six images were created with the built-in image-generation workflow. The canonical image was generated first; every later image used it as the identity reference.

## Permanent identity block

Depict the exact same fictional English home cricket ground. Preserve **The Eleven Arches**, a curved Victorian red-brick railway viaduct following the northern boundary, with ivy, a working local rail line and a broad central arch containing a blank traditional manual scoreboard. Preserve the two-storey dark pavilion-green timber and warm red-brick clubhouse, compact English hill town, ridges, pitch and boundary geography. These are permanent architectural facts, not optional motifs. Keep the ground modest, old and loved rather than derelict or stadium-like.

Use an original premium cinematic-anime environmental style: precise observational drawing, painterly luminous weather, crisp forms, atmospheric depth, sophisticated colour and an adult sports-drama tone. Do not imitate a named artist, studio, film, game or franchise. Avoid photorealism, childish proportions, 3D rendering, generic mobile-game fantasy and glossy gacha art.

All scenes are 16:9 full-bleed backgrounds. Reserve the top 7% for navigation. Keep the lower-left and lower-right quarters comparatively calm and darker for white homepage UI. Do not bake in interface, text, numbers, club badges, logos or watermarks. People must be small generic club figures with no prominent Gaffer or recognisable faces. Cricket geometry, equipment and poses must be physically credible.

## Variant prompts

### 1. Sunbreak match — canonical

Late-spring home match shortly after a shower, viewed from just outside the south-west boundary. The pitch runs diagonally; pavilion sits left; viaduct fills the upper middle distance; small local train crosses. Wet leaves and puddle glints, monumental pearl-blue clouds, warm sun shafts and long soft shadows. Small generic players in whites and a modest mixed-age crowd.

### 2. Dawn mist

Spring dawn from beneath the north-eastern outer arch, looking south-west toward the pavilion. Near brick arch frames the upper-right while the viaduct curves away. Pale lemon sunrise under blue-grey cloud, ribbons of mist, sparkling dew and warm pavilion lamps. A few volunteers roll the square while distant players stretch.

### 3. Summer noon

High summer clear midday from the pavilion veranda, looking toward the viaduct. One green veranda post and ornate eave frame the left. Brilliant cobalt sky, clean towering cumulus, short shadows, saturated grass, club fifty-over match and sun-hatted spectators. Blank scoreboard stays beneath the broad central arch.

### 4. Storm front

Early-autumn match from a low south-east long-off boundary angle. The viaduct recedes dramatically across the background and the pavilion is smaller at far left. Slate storm front, wind-driven leaves, bending trees, rain curtains over hills and one final warm blade of light across the pitch. Volunteers ready covers while play continues.

### 5. Blue-hour cup match

Blue hour from the eastern town-side boundary, looking west. Four modest community floodlight masts illuminate the square; pavilion lamps and train windows glow amber. Cobalt-to-apricot sky and an active cup match with generic players in deep navy/burnt-orange versus cream/green. Keep the scale intimate, not stadium-like.

### 6. Quiet night after rain

Late night from the shelter of the pavilion veranda. Wet paving and ornate green ironwork frame the right. Deep indigo sky, broken cloud, stars, moonlit puddles, covers on the square and warm windows. The viaduct and train glow softly. Only volunteers stacking chairs and one distant player leaving with a kit bag remain.
